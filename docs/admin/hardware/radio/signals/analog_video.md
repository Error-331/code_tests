# Analog video

Analog video signals are defined by temporal(временные) and spatial(пространственные) parameters, including: 

- resolution (480i-1080p); 
- frame rates (25–60 Hz), 
- scanning methods (interlaced or progressive);
- aspect ratios; 

Key signals use sync pulses (H-Sync/V-Sync) for timing, with bandwidths ranging from 4.2 MHz (NTSC) to over 25 MHz (HDTV). The main formats include composite (CVBS) and component (YUV/RGB). 

## Key Analog Video Parameters

Scanning Systems:

- Interlaced (NTSC, PAL) - odd and even lines are scanned in two separate fields, which are then combined to form a complete frame;
- Progressive (VGA) - all lines are scanned sequentially in one pass;

Resolution and Aspect Ratio:

- Vertical Resolution - defined by the number of active scanning lines (e.g., 525 for NTSC, 625 for PAL);
- Horizontal Resolution - limited by the signal bandwidth;
- Aspect Ratio - Standard definition is typically 4:3, while high definition is 16:9;

Signal Timing:

- Frame/Field Rate - the speed at which frames (25–30 Hz) or fields (50–60 Hz) are refreshed, affecting motion smoothness;
- Horizontal Rate - number of lines scanned per second (e.g., approx. 15.6 kHz for PAL, 15.7 kHz for NTSC);
- Sync Pulses - H-Sync controls line-by-line scanning, while V-Sync manages field-by-field scanning;

Signal Types:

- Composite (CVBS) - combines luminance, chrominance, and synchronization into one channel (e.g., NTSC, PAL);
- Component (YUV, YPbPr, RGB) - separates signals for higher quality, commonly used in high-definition analog;

Bandwidth: 

- NTSC - approx. 4.2 MHz;
- PAL - approx. 5.0–5.5 MHz;
- HDTV/HD-TVI - up to 25 MHz or higher, capable of 720p/1080p resolutions;

## Key Standards Characteristics

- NTSC - 525 lines, 59.94 fields/s, 3.58 MHz color subcarrier;
- PAL - 625 lines, 50 fields/s, 4.43 MHz color subcarrier;
- Analog HD (AHD/TVI/CVI) - enables resolutions up to 4K over coaxial cable;
